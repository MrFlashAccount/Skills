# Lifecycle Email Reference

Use for warm email sequences tied to product, customer, or campaign lifecycle moments.

## Use when

- welcome and activation sequences
- onboarding or setup-support emails
- nurture drips after signup, content download, trial, webinar, or demo interest
- upgrade, expansion, renewal, win-back, and re-engagement flows
- launch follow-up and campaign sequences
- lifecycle email audits where timing, segmentation, or message hierarchy are weak

## Do not use when

- the audience is net-new cold outbound
- in-product onboarding is the main artifact and email is secondary
- the main asset is a standalone landing page

## Inputs to gather

- entry trigger
- what the recipient already knows or has done
- next best action and what blocks it
- proof, assets, or product truths that can move them forward
- send constraints, suppression rules, exit conditions, and channel overlap

## Workflow

1. Classify the lifecycle motion.
2. Map entry trigger, audience state, desired conversion, and exit conditions.
3. Design the sequence arc with one job per email.
4. Draft the sequence overview and per-email subject, goal, CTA, and copy.
5. Check helpfulness, stage fit, channel coordination, and claim honesty.
6. Package experiments, missing data, and required handoffs.

## Rules

- Sequence around the recipient's stage, not internal calendar habits.
- One email, one job.
- Match cadence to urgency.
- Coordinate with product, support, and sales touches.
- Keep proof and claims grounded in current reality.
- Preserve segmentation gaps and unknowns explicitly.
