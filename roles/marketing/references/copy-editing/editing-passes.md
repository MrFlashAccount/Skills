# Editing Passes

Run the relevant passes in order. Not every job needs every pass, but proof and audience fit should be considered on every non-trivial edit.

## 1. Clarity

Check:
- buried ledes
- overloaded sentences
- jargon without buyer meaning
- unclear references and weak transitions

## 2. Voice and audience fit

Check:
- tone consistency
- whether the language sounds like the intended buyer, not the internal team
- whether sophistication matches channel and audience awareness

## 3. So-what and stakes

Check:
- benefits attached to features
- consequences of the problem or status quo
- whether the copy answers why this matters now

## 4. Proof and claim discipline

Check:
- unsupported metrics or superlatives
- social proof without attribution or specificity
- competitive claims that outrun evidence
- promises stronger than current product truth

## 5. Specificity and differentiation

Check:
- vague value language
- generic category copy competitors could all say
- missing concrete examples, timeframes, or mechanisms

## 6. Friction and CTA readiness

Check:
- unanswered objections near the ask
- unclear next step
- hidden commitment, risk, or switching anxiety
- missing trust signals where the ask is strongest

## Pass criteria

The edit is ready when:
- the audience still fits
- the strongest remaining claims are supportable
- the CTA feels earned
- the revised copy is cleaner without becoming blander or less true
