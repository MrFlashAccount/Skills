# Customer Research Workflow

## 1. Frame the decision

Name the downstream decision first:
- sharpen positioning
- validate or split ICPs
- build personas or JTBD
- improve a page or campaign
- understand churn, losses, or objections
- find proof gaps before launch

If the decision is fuzzy, stop the work from becoming random quote collection.

## 2. Inventory evidence

Check what already exists before requesting new collection:
- interview transcripts
- sales calls and demos
- support tickets
- surveys and NPS responses
- win/loss and churn notes
- review sites and community posts
- internal docs with prior GTM assumptions

Record for each source:
- segment
- date range
- source bias
- sample size
- trust level

## 3. Split the work mode

### Analyze existing
Use when raw materials already exist.

Extract per item:
- JTBD: functional, emotional, social
- trigger event
- pain and stakes
- desired outcome
- objections and fears
- alternatives considered
- exact vocabulary
- proof expectations

### Gather new
Use when evidence is thin or stale.

Prioritize channels by ICP:
- review sites for category patterns and competitor complaints
- Reddit/forums/community threads for unfiltered pain language
- sales/support/churn conversations for buying friction and switching anxiety
- job posts / LinkedIn / industry communities for trigger events and maturity signals

## 4. Tag and score findings

For every finding, keep:
- source
- date
- segment
- theme
- confidence: high / medium / low
- evidence type: quote / summary / inference

Confidence guidance:
- **High**: repeated across 3+ independent sources or segments where relevant
- **Medium**: repeated but limited in recency, sample, or source variety
- **Low**: single-source or speculative; useful only as a lead to validate

## 5. Build synthesis layers

### ICP / segment layer
For each meaningful segment, state:
- who they are
- what context they buy in
- why the pain is acute now
- what alternative they are replacing
- what would make them a bad fit

### JTBD layer
State:
- main job they hire the product for
- success criteria in their words
- emotional relief or social outcome they care about

### Objection layer
Map:
- trust objections
- switch-cost objections
- pricing objections
- feature-fit objections
- internal-buy-in objections

### Proof layer
List what evidence would earn belief:
- concrete results
- customer examples
- demo-able product truths
- testimonials or references
- implementation/support assurances

### Claim-discipline layer
For each major messaging direction, note:
- safe claim now
- claim needing more evidence
- claim not supportable from current research

## 6. Produce only the needed artifact

Choose based on the decision:
- research synthesis for strategy choices
- VOC quote bank for copy inputs
- persona/ICP brief for targeting
- JTBD map for messaging/product alignment
- objections + proof matrix for launch and sales
- research-gap memo when evidence is not yet sufficient

## 7. Handoff cleanly

Every output should include:
- top findings
- strongest quotes or evidence
- contradictions
- unsupported-but-tempting claims to avoid
- open questions
- recommended downstream owner: positioning, copy, launch, or sales enablement
