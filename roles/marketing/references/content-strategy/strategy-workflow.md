# Strategy Workflow

## 1. Ground the plan

Start from:
- audience / ICP
- jobs to be done
- pains and stakes
- alternatives and objections
- proof the market will believe

If the task is really about developer docs or README teaching, route out.

## 2. Build the content map

Map ideas across:
- audience segment
- buyer stage
- problem or objection
- channel / format
- proof source or evidence needed
- CTA or downstream action

## 3. Choose the mix

Use two buckets:
- **search capture**: content for explicit demand, buyer questions, comparisons, implementation friction, and recurring objections
- **demand creation**: content for narrative shifts, strong viewpoints, original insights, launch framing, and category education

Do not force every topic into one content type. Pick the cheapest honest format that fits the job.

## 4. Prioritize

Rank by:
- audience pain intensity
- business relevance
- proof strength or proof feasibility
- channel fit
- production cost
- urgency or launch timing

## 5. Review attack

Pressure-test:
- topic list too broad for one audience
- channels chosen by habit instead of buyer behavior
- content ideas with no proof path
- strategy that is really a writing backlog with no prioritization logic
- DevRel/docs topics accidentally mixed into marketing content

## 6. Final review

Make sure the plan explains:
- what to create
- why it matters
- who it is for
- where it should ship
- what proof or inputs are needed
- what to do first
