# Competitor Profiling Reference

Use for structured competitor dossiers and comparison-ready competitive inputs.

## Use when

- competitor landscape framing from named competitors or URLs
- structured competitor profiles with traceable evidence
- comparison-ready notes for messaging, launch, or sales work
- strengths, weaknesses, threats, and opportunity framing

## Do not use when

- the main job is final public comparison-page copy
- the task is customer-side VOC or persona work without a competitive evidence need
- the output would rely on speculative or legally risky assertions

## Workflow

1. Lock the competitor list and the decision this research must support.
2. Load shared GTM context so comparison stays grounded in real product truth.
3. Gather evidence from sites, pricing, onboarding surfaces, reviews, and other available signals.
4. Normalize each competitor into the same profile shape.
5. Extract differentiators, audience clues, proof, objections, and switch triggers.
6. Separate verified facts, informed inferences, and unknowns.
7. Deliver profiles plus a cross-competitor summary or matrix.

## Deliverables

- competitor profile
- landscape summary
- comparison-ready matrix
- differentiation and objection brief
- page-priority recommendation for downstream comparison work

## Rules

- Facts over dunking.
- Cite the source type for every material claim.
- Separate current facts from stale copy and speculation.
- Be honest where the competitor is stronger.
- Compare against the status quo too, not only named vendors.
- Flag where your own product lacks proof or parity.
