# Launch Reference

Use for launch planning and launch messaging orchestration.

## Use when

- deciding what is launching, for whom, and why now
- choosing launch type, rollout shape, and channel sequence
- framing messages across announcement surfaces
- defining the launch asset list, owner handoffs, and proof requirements
- planning pre-launch, launch-day, and post-launch follow-through

## Do not use when

- the main job is developer-facing technical launch framing only
- the task is setup, migration, or feature-usage docs
- unresolved customer research or pricing design is the real blocker

## Task classes

- `update`: small launch adjustment with the same audience, offer, and channel strategy
- `full-cycle`: a new launch or any material change to rollout shape, audience, or proof strategy

## Workflow

- `update`: compact launch contract -> reconfirm launch type, audience, proof, constraints -> revise plan -> check handoffs
- `full-cycle`: contract -> classify launch and rollout shape -> map audience, promise, proof, objections, risks -> build channel and asset sequence -> identify cross-functional handoffs -> rubric review

## Output shape

- Launch type
- Audience / fit
- What is new now
- Why it matters now
- Primary promise
- Proof / claim boundaries
- Risks / objections
- Channel sequence
- Asset list
- Owner handoffs
- Open questions

## Rules

- Keep launch scope tied to current product reality, not roadmap hope.
- Name who the launch is for and who it is not for.
- Separate announcement story from education/onboarding work.
- Use channels as a system, not isolated spikes.
- Every strong claim needs proof, an example, or an explicit caveat.
