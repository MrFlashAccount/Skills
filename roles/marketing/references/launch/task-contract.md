# Launch contract

Build a short contract before drafting the plan.

## Required fields

- `What is launching`: product, feature, integration, package, program, or availability change
- `Launch type`: private alpha, beta, early access, GA, relaunch, expansion, or announcement-only update
- `Audience`: who should care now, and who should not be targeted yet
- `Goal`: awareness, waitlist, activation, adoption, expansion, pipeline, or retention
- `Why now`: the time-sensitive reason this deserves attention now
- `Proof points`: demos, validated capabilities, customer evidence, screenshots, or explicit constraints
- `Key objections`: adoption, trust, fit, switching, pricing, or timing concerns
- `Channels`: owned, borrowed, rented, and product-native surfaces that are realistically available
- `Dependencies`: docs, DevRel, sales, support, research, product, legal, or launch ops blockers
- `Risk points`: overclaim risk, missing proof, launch timing risk, unclear audience, weak onboarding, or channel fragility

## Compact template

- What is launching / Launch type
- Audience / Goal
- Why now
- Proof / Constraints
- Channels / Assets
- Dependencies
- Risks / Unknowns

## Critique prompts

- Is this a real launch moment or just a changelog item?
- Who will care enough to act now?
- What promise would become embarrassing if the product underdelivers?
- Which asset creates interest, and which asset helps the prospect succeed after the click?
- What must be learned before scaling the launch wider?
