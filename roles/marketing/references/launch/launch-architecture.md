# Launch architecture

Use this to keep launch planning structured and honest.

## 1. Pick the right launch shape

Choose the smallest launch that matches product truth.

- `private alpha`: learning with close guidance, low external promise
- `beta`: broader access with explicit rough edges and feedback loops
- `early access`: controlled commercial or near-commercial rollout with gated access
- `GA`: broad availability with repeatable onboarding and support readiness
- `relaunch / expansion`: same product, new segment, packaging, channel, or availability story

If onboarding, proof, or support is still shaky, do not force GA framing.

## 2. Plan across three channel buckets

- `owned`: website, email, blog, product surfaces, webinars, community
- `borrowed`: partners, customers, guests, newsletters, podcasts, communities you do not own
- `rented`: social platforms, launch directories, app marketplaces, paid distribution

Owned channels capture value. Borrowed and rented channels create reach. Plan how attention flows back into owned surfaces.

## 3. Sequence the launch

### Pre-launch
- tighten audience and promise
- line up proof, screenshots, demos, and FAQs
- prepare capture and follow-up paths
- brief sales/support/CS on what is changing

### Launch window
- publish the core announcement
- activate the highest-fit channels first
- respond quickly to questions and objections
- monitor friction in signup, onboarding, and messaging comprehension

### Follow-through
- convert launch traffic into onboarding and education
- publish follow-up proof, FAQs, and comparisons as needed
- capture objections and feedback for pricing, sales, docs, and product loops
- decide whether to extend, repeat, narrow, or stop the rollout

## 4. Match the asset set to the launch

Typical launch assets:
- primary announcement or landing page
- email or in-product announcement
- short social / community variants
- FAQ or objection note
- demo, screenshots, or product tour
- internal enablement brief for sales/support/success

Do not create more surfaces than the team can maintain during the launch window.

## 5. Pressure-test before scaling

Ask:
- Is the promised outcome obvious?
- Does the proof actually support the promise?
- Will interested users know what to do next?
- Is there a plan for objections, support load, and launch-day questions?
- Which unknowns should stay open and be learned in beta instead of hidden in GA copy?
