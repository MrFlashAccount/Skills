# Pricing Reference

Use for pricing, packaging, monetization, and pricing-message strategy.

## Use when

- choosing pricing structure, tiers, and packaging
- selecting or pressure-testing a pricing metric
- deciding between free plan, trial, hybrid, or sales-led packaging
- framing pricing-page or pricing-conversation messages
- evaluating price increase, grandfathering, or rollout options

## Do not use when

- the main job is only paywall copy or upgrade UX
- there is no pricing decision behind the work
- the task is deep research without a pricing decision frame

## Task classes

- `tune`: local pricing, packaging, or messaging adjustment within an existing model
- `full-cycle`: new pricing model, new packaging logic, major price change, or first pricing strategy

## Workflow

- `tune`: pricing contract -> confirm segment, goal, constraints -> adjust tier, metric, or message -> check rollout and handoffs
- `full-cycle`: contract -> clarify pricing goal -> choose packaging shape, value metric, and price architecture -> map proof, objections, and adoption friction -> plan communication and rollout -> review risk and claim discipline

## Output shape

- Pricing goal
- Target segment
- Current model / constraints
- Recommended packaging
- Value metric
- Price architecture
- Message / proof strategy
- Objections / risks
- Rollout recommendation
- Downstream handoffs
- Open questions

## Rules

- Price to value and market reality, not internal preference.
- Separate packaging, metric, and price point.
- Keep plan names and tier boundaries easy to explain.
- Tie premium claims to features, service levels, or proof that exist now.
- Recommend tests or narrower rollout when evidence is weak.
