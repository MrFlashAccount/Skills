# Pricing contract

Build a compact contract before changing pricing or packaging.

## Required fields

- `Product / offer`: what is being sold now
- `Target segment`: who this price is for, and who it is not for
- `Current model`: tiers, metric, price points, discounts, trial/free plan, enterprise motion
- `Goal`: adoption, conversion quality, ARPU, expansion, profitability, enterprise capture, or simplification
- `Value basis`: why customers pay, what outcome scales, and what alternatives exist
- `Evidence`: customer research, usage patterns, sales feedback, win/loss notes, competitive context, support tickets, or explicit constraints
- `Decision scope`: packaging, metric, price point, rollout, page messaging, or all of the above
- `Dependencies`: billing, product limits, sales motion, docs, legal review, launch timing, or contracts
- `Risk points`: sticker shock, upgrade confusion, hidden usage cliffs, cannibalization, support burden, or proof gaps

## Compact template

- Product / Segment / Goal
- Current model / Constraints
- Value basis / Evidence
- Decision scope
- Risks / Dependencies / Unknowns

## Critique prompts

- What customer behavior is this pricing model trying to encourage?
- Is the metric aligned with delivered value or just convenient to charge for?
- Which tier boundary will confuse or anger a good-fit customer?
- What evidence is real, and what is assumption dressed up as insight?
- If this price changed tomorrow, what would sales, support, and docs need to explain differently?
