# Pricing rollout and handoffs

Pricing work fails when the model changes but the explanation does not.

## Rollout options

Choose the smallest rollout that matches confidence.

- `quiet test`: limited page or segment experiment when evidence is still thin
- `new customers only`: safer default for many price increases
- `grandfathered change`: protects trust while improving new-business pricing
- `time-boxed transition`: clear notice period for existing customers moving to a new model
- `launch-linked rollout`: pricing change ships alongside a meaningful packaging or product change

State:
- who is affected
- when it changes
- what stays the same
- what the buyer gets for the price
- what support path exists for questions

## Sales handoff

Route to `sales-enablement` when reps need:
- pricing overview slides or one-pagers
- packaging talk tracks by persona
- objection handling for budget, timing, or competitor pricing
- ROI framing that stays within available proof

Provide:
- approved tier logic
- fit boundaries by segment
- discount or exception rules if they exist
- proof points and explicit no-claim zones

## Launch handoff

Route to `launch` when pricing is part of a broader market moment.

Provide:
- what changed in packaging or availability
- why the market should care now
- what proof supports the new offer
- which audience segments should hear about it first

## Docs and product handoff

Route to `docs-writer` or product docs owners when pricing changes affect:
- plan comparison docs
- upgrade or billing explanation
- usage-limit behavior
- migration guidance between plans

Provide:
- exact plan names
- feature and limit boundaries
- upgrade/downgrade rules
- known confusion risks

## Customer-research handoff

Open customer-research work when you still need to answer:
- who will pay and why
- where willingness to pay breaks
- which feature or service tier actually drives premium demand
- why prospects stall at a pricing page or pricing conversation

If a reusable packet is needed first, use `research-critic` to structure the evidence gaps and decisions blocked.
