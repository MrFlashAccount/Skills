# Sales Enablement Reference

Use for buyer-facing collateral, objection handling, and rep-usable messaging.

## Use when

- creating or revising decks, one-pagers, leave-behinds, proposals, demo narratives, or champion materials
- turning positioning into rep-usable talk tracks
- building objection handling tied to proof and fit
- tailoring collateral by persona, stage, segment, or package
- preparing collateral for launch follow-through or pricing conversations

## Do not use when

- the main job is deciding the pricing model itself
- the work is launch orchestration across channels
- the artifact is developer-facing README or technical docs

## Task classes

- `asset`: a bounded asset or localized revision
- `system`: a new sales message set, multi-asset pack, or major collateral refresh across personas or stages

## Workflow

- `asset`: contract -> confirm audience, deal stage, desired action -> draft around one message spine and minimum proof -> check objections and handoffs
- `system`: contract -> define pain, differentiation, proof, and next step -> choose minimum asset set -> build outlines and objection coverage -> identify launch, pricing, DevRel, docs, and research dependencies -> review for scan speed and usability

## Output shape

- Audience / stage
- Asset or system goal
- Message spine
- Recommended assets
- Proof to include
- Objections to cover
- Customization notes
- Cross-functional handoffs
- Open questions

## Rules

- Make the asset usable in live sales contexts; scannable beats comprehensive.
- Tie every differentiator to buyer impact.
- Give champions material they can forward internally.
- Keep persona differences explicit.
- Do not invent ROI math, customer quotes, competitor claims, or implementation promises.
- Lower claim strength when proof is weak.
