# Asset frameworks

Use these frameworks to keep sales collateral focused.

## 1. Message spine first

Before choosing format, lock four things:
- the buyer problem in buyer language
- the differentiator that matters for this audience
- the proof that makes the claim believable
- the next action this asset should drive

If the spine is fuzzy, the collateral will become generic.

## 2. Minimal asset guidance

### Deck
Use when a live narrative is needed.
Recommended structure:
1. current problem
2. cost or risk of current state
3. why change now
4. your approach
5. product or workflow proof
6. customer or operational proof
7. implementation / rollout reality
8. pricing or commercial frame when appropriate
9. next step

### One-pager / leave-behind
Use when someone needs a fast summary or an internal-forwardable note.
Keep:
- one audience
- one problem frame
- three differentiators max
- one proof block
- one next step

### Objection sheet
Use when reps need live-call retrieval.
For each objection include:
- what the buyer says
- what concern sits underneath it
- short response approach
- proof or caveat
- follow-up question

### Demo or talk track
Use when the product must be shown, not just described.
Map each section to a buyer pain and an interaction point.
Do not show features without a reason the buyer should care.

## 3. Customize by buyer type

- `economic buyer`: cost, risk, payback logic, operational impact
- `technical buyer`: architecture, security, integration, implementation truth
- `champion`: internal selling language, peer proof, next-step clarity
- `end user`: workflow fit, ease, time saved, daily friction removed

## 4. Keep the asset honest

If a proof point is missing, choose one:
- weaken the claim
- swap in product truth instead of outcome rhetoric
- mark the proof as needed before this asset goes live
