# Proof, objections, and handoffs

## Objection handling standard

For every major objection, capture:
- `what they say`
- `what they actually mean`
- `best available response`
- `proof or caveat`
- `next question`

Common categories:
- price / budget
- timing / priority
- status quo / switching pain
- competition / alternatives
- security / integration / technical fit
- procurement / rollout risk

Do not answer objections with confidence theater. If the proof is not there, say what must be confirmed.

## Proof hierarchy

Use the strongest available evidence first:
1. verified customer result or quote
2. product capability with screenshot, demo, or technical confirmation
3. operational fact such as implementation steps or support model
4. directional hypothesis labeled clearly as unproven

Avoid using vague superlatives or invented benchmarks to fill proof gaps.

## Launch handoff

Pull from `launch` when the collateral depends on:
- what is actually new
- launch audience sequencing
- launch-specific FAQs
- channel-specific CTAs or timing

## Pricing handoff

Pull from `pricing` when the collateral depends on:
- plan names and package boundaries
- discount rules or rollout terms
- how to explain price differences by segment
- budget and ROI objections tied to the pricing model

## DevRel and docs handoff

Route to `devrel-copywriter` or `docs-writer` when sales assets expose gaps in:
- developer-first product framing
- setup, implementation, or migration explanation
- technical proof needed by evaluators
- activation steps promised during a sale

## Customer-research handoff

Open customer-research work when reps keep hearing objections or loss reasons that the current messaging cannot explain.
Capture:
- repeated objection wording
- which persona says it
- where in the funnel it appears
- what decision it blocks

Use `research-critic` first if the repo needs a structured evidence-gap packet before broader research work.
