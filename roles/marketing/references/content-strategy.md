# Content Strategy Reference

Use for market-facing content planning.

## Use when

- content pillars and topic clusters
- editorial calendars and backlogs
- launch-content planning
- search-led or demand-creation prioritization
- objection-handling and sales-enablement content plans

## Do not use when

- the main job is writing final page copy
- the asset already exists and just needs editing
- the job is repository README/docs planning

## Task classes

- `idea-pack`: a short list of ideas for a defined audience/problem
- `roadmap`: a prioritized plan across pillars, stages, or channels
- `program`: end-to-end strategy with goals, audience, themes, formats, distribution, proof, and measurement hooks

## Workflow

- `idea-pack`: contract -> topic filter -> shortlist -> final packaging
- `roadmap`: contract -> audience/problem map -> theme and stage map -> prioritize -> review
- `program`: contract -> GTM grounding -> audience/JTBD map -> search vs demand-creation mix -> channel fit -> prioritization -> critic attack -> revise

## Rules

- Start from shared GTM context before choosing themes or angles.
- Every topic must connect to a real pain, job, objection, or buying question.
- Name the channel and distribution assumption.
- Tie claims and topic angles to evidence or a plan for proof collection.
- Distinguish search capture from demand creation.
- Prefer a small prioritized plan over a giant idea dump.
