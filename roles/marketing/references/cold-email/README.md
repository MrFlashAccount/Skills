# Cold Email Reference

Use for outbound email to people who did not ask to hear from you.

## Use when

- first-touch cold emails
- founder-led outreach
- SDR / AE prospecting emails
- outbound follow-up sequences
- subject-line and CTA iteration
- account or segment-specific outbound angle testing
- teardown of underperforming cold emails

## Do not use when

- lifecycle, onboarding, nurture, re-engagement, or trigger-based flows
- landing pages or broader sales collateral as the main artifact

## Inputs to gather

- target buyer and company shape
- trigger or research signal
- painful before-state
- concrete value bridge to the offer
- proof, credibility, or believable constraint
- desired reply motion and compliance or brand limits

## Workflow

1. Classify the ask: single email, short sequence, teardown, or angle pack.
2. Build the outbound brief: buyer, trigger, problem hypothesis, safe proof, low-friction ask.
3. Choose the shape: observation -> problem -> proof -> ask, trigger -> implication -> proof -> ask, or another simple shape.
4. Draft the asset: subject options, email body, and distinct follow-up angles when needed.
5. Quality gate: human, specific, one ask, no fake urgency or generic praise.
6. Package testing notes, missing proof, and any sales/docs/DevRel handoffs.

## Output shapes

- outreach brief
- subject-line options
- first-touch email
- follow-up sequence with per-touch angle and delay
- rewrite notes
- missing proof or research gaps

## Rules

- Keep the email short enough to justify the interruption.
- Lead with their world, not your company bio.
- Personalization only counts if it changes the reason for reaching out.
- Prefer interest-based asks over premature meeting grabs.
- Each follow-up must add a new angle, not a bump.
- Never invent customer names, numbers, or certainty.
