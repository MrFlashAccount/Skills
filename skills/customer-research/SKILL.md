# Customer Research

Use this skill when the job is to reduce customer evidence into usable go-to-market truth.

Load shared context before making positioning judgments.

## Own

- evidence collection and research-plan shaping
- synthesis of interviews, calls, reviews, tickets, surveys, and community/forum data
- ICP, persona, and JTBD extraction grounded in evidence
- pains, stakes, triggers, alternatives, objections, and desired-outcome mapping
- proof-point and claim-support discovery
- customer-language capture for downstream messaging
- explicit open-questions and research-gap reporting

## Do not own

- competitor site dossier creation from URLs; use `competitor-profiling`
- final copywriting, launch execution, or sales collateral production
- inventing persona traits, proof, or market certainty from weak samples
- product strategy decisions disguised as research certainty

## Default workflow

1. Clarify the decision the research needs to support.
2. Inventory available evidence before asking for more.
3. Separate `analyze-existing` from `gather-new` work; many tasks use both.
4. Tag findings by segment, source, date, and confidence.
5. Extract ICP/JTBD, pains, triggers, outcomes, objections, proof signals, alternatives, and exact language.
6. Synthesize patterns, contradictions, and gaps.
7. Deliver the requested artifact and route the findings downstream.

## Rules

- Evidence before messaging.
- Keep segment boundaries explicit; do not average unlike buyers into one fake persona.
- Capture exact customer wording when it materially shapes claims or copy.
- Weight recent evidence more heavily than stale evidence.
- Separate frequency from intensity; a loud outlier is not the market.
- Label confidence and sample bias.
- Keep current product reality separate from what customers wish existed.
- Call out unsupported claims before they leak into positioning.

## Questions to resolve early

- What decision should this research improve: positioning, ICP, launch, page messaging, objections, sales enablement, or product feedback?
- What evidence already exists locally?
- Which segment matters most?
- Is the job synthesis only, fresh evidence gathering, or both?
- What deliverable is needed?

## Deliverables

Use the smallest artifact that unblocks the decision:

- research synthesis
- VOC quote bank
- ICP / persona summary
- JTBD map
- objection and proof-point matrix
- research-gap memo

## Handoffs

- `competitor-profiling`: pair customer evidence with competitive evidence
- messaging / positioning skill: turn findings into positioning and message hierarchy
- content / copy skill: use VOC, objections, and proof in assets
- launch skill: use pains, stakes, proof, and segments for rollout framing
- sales-enablement skill: use objections, alternatives, and proof for rep-facing material
