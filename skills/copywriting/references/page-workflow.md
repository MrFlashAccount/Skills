# Page and Surface Workflow

## 1. Fit check

Confirm:
- who this surface is for
- why they are here now
- what they already believe
- what action they should take next

If the artifact is actually developer-facing README/docs framing, route out to `devrel-copywriter` or `docs-writer`.

## 2. Message hierarchy

Build a compact ladder:
1. primary promise
2. support message 1: problem or stakes
3. support message 2: differentiated mechanism or approach
4. support message 3: proof or trust builder
5. objection answer
6. CTA

## 3. Surface plan

Choose only the sections the channel needs.

Typical landing-page order:
1. headline
2. subheadline
3. primary CTA
4. proof bar or proof block
5. problem / stakes
6. solution / benefits
7. mechanism or how-it-works
8. objections / FAQ
9. final CTA

Typical ad/social order:
1. hook
2. pain or desire
3. differentiated angle
4. proof or specificity
5. CTA

Typical sales-collateral order:
1. buyer pain / context
2. value promise
3. why this over alternatives
4. proof
5. next step

## 4. Draft

Draft to the channel, not to a generic page template.
- short surfaces need density and fast payoff
- longer pages need section logic and non-repetitive proof
- keep benefit claims tied to believable product truth

## 5. Marketing critic attack

Pressure-test the draft for:
- vague audience or mixed audiences
- headline promise stronger than the proof
- weak differentiation or generic category copy
- objection gaps near the CTA
- channel mismatch in length, tone, or ask

## 6. Final review

Before delivery, check:
- strongest promise appears early
- proof supports the hardest claims
- every section earns its place
- CTA is specific about what happens next
- assumptions and open proof gaps stay visible
