# Content Strategy

Use this skill as the planning harness for market-facing content programs.

The Marketing role supplies audience, positioning, and proof judgment. This skill turns that into a content plan, topic system, and prioritized output package.

## Routing boundary

Use this skill when the task is deciding what market-facing content to create, for whom, why, and in what order.

Use it for:
- content pillars and topic clusters
- editorial calendars and content backlogs
- launch-content planning
- search-led or demand-creation content prioritization
- objection-handling and sales-enablement content plans

Do not use it for:
- writing the final landing-page or campaign copy; use `copywriting`
- editing an existing page or email asset; use `copy-editing`
- repository README/docs planning, onboarding docs, tutorials, or API docs; use `devrel-copywriter` or `docs-writer`

## Task class

- `idea-pack`: short list of content ideas or angles for a defined audience/problem.
- `roadmap`: prioritized plan across pillars, stages, or channels.
- `program`: end-to-end strategy covering goals, audience, themes, formats, distribution assumptions, proof, and measurement hooks.

If the request affects prioritization, audience, or distribution choices, do not reduce it to a loose brainstorm.

## Workflow

- `idea-pack`: contract -> topic filter -> shortlist -> final packaging.
- `roadmap`: contract -> audience/problem map -> theme and stage map -> prioritize -> review -> final packaging.
- `program`: contract -> evidence and GTM grounding -> audience/JTBD map -> searchable vs demand-creation mix -> channel fit -> prioritization -> critic attack -> revise -> final packaging.

## Rules

- Treat shared GTM context as required foundation before deciding content themes or angles.
- Keep market-facing content strategy separate from DevRel/docs planning.
- Every topic should connect to a real audience pain, job, objection, or buying question.
- Name the channel and distribution assumption; a blog post, partner asset, sales leave-behind, and lifecycle email play different roles.
- Tie claims and topic angles to evidence, proof potential, or a plan for proof collection.
- Distinguish search capture from demand creation instead of mixing them lazily.
- Prefer a smaller prioritized plan over a giant undifferentiated idea dump.
- Keep open questions explicit when proof, volume, or audience data is missing.
