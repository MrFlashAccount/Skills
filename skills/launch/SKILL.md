Use this skill for launch planning and launch messaging orchestration.

Load shared GTM context before making launch judgments, then apply the Marketing role contract.

## Routing boundary

Use this skill when the job is:
- deciding what is launching, for whom, and why it matters now
- choosing launch type, rollout shape, and channel sequence
- framing launch messages across announcement surfaces
- defining the launch asset list, owner handoffs, and proof requirements
- planning pre-launch, launch-day, and post-launch follow-through

Do not use this skill when the main job is:
- writing repository `readme` openings or developer-facing launch framing for technical audiences only; use `devrel-copywriter`
- teaching setup, migration, or feature usage; use `docs-writer`
- deep research synthesis, interview analysis, or unresolved ICP discovery; use `research-critic` or direct customer-research work first
- pricing/package design; use `pricing`
- sales collateral production; use `sales-enablement`

## Task class

- `update`: a small launch adjustment that keeps the same audience, offer, and channel strategy
- `full-cycle`: a new launch, a materially different audience or positioning angle, or any change to rollout shape, asset map, or proof strategy

If unsure, treat it as `full-cycle`.

## Workflow

### Update

1. Build the compact launch contract.
2. Reconfirm launch type, audience, proof, and constraints.
3. Revise the plan or messaging slice.
4. Check handoffs and open questions before finalizing.

### Full-cycle

1. Build the launch contract from product reality and GTM context.
2. Classify the launch and choose the rollout shape.
3. Map audience, promise, proof, objections, and launch risks.
4. Build the channel and asset sequence across pre-launch, launch, and follow-through.
5. Identify cross-functional handoffs to DevRel, docs, sales, support, and customer research.
6. Review against the Marketing rubric before finalizing.

## Output contract

Return a launch packet with only the sections needed for the ask:
- `Launch type`
- `Audience / fit`
- `What is new now`
- `Why it matters now`
- `Primary promise`
- `Proof / claim boundaries`
- `Risks / objections`
- `Channel sequence`
- `Asset list`
- `Owner handoffs`
- `Open questions`

## Rules

- Keep launch scope tied to current product reality, not roadmap hope.
- Name who the launch is for and who it is not for.
- Separate the announcement story from the education/onboarding work needed after interest is created.
- Use channels as a system; do not treat a single spike channel as the whole launch.
- Every strong claim needs proof, a concrete example, or an explicit caveat.
- Do not invent customer demand, traction, testimonials, or performance metrics.
- Do not promise legal, procurement, security, or implementation outcomes that require another owner to confirm.
- When product truth, audience fit, or proof is thin, downgrade the launch ambition and surface the gap.
