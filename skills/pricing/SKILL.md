Use this skill for monetization and pricing strategy work grounded in product truth and customer evidence.

Load shared GTM context before making packaging or pricing judgments, then apply the Marketing role contract.

## Routing boundary

Use this skill when the job is:
- choosing pricing structure, tiers, and packaging
- selecting or pressure-testing a pricing metric
- deciding between free plan, trial, hybrid, or sales-led packaging
- framing pricing-page or pricing-conversation messages
- evaluating price increase, grandfathering, or rollout options
- identifying evidence gaps that block credible pricing decisions

Do not use this skill when the main job is:
- building paywall copy or in-product upgrade UX only
- writing generic website copy without a pricing decision behind it
- producing full sales collateral; use `sales-enablement`
- launch orchestration across channels; use `launch`
- deep willingness-to-pay research synthesis or interview interpretation without a pricing decision frame; use customer-research work first

## Task class

- `tune`: a local pricing, packaging, or messaging adjustment within an existing model
- `full-cycle`: a new pricing model, new packaging logic, a major price change, or a first pricing strategy for the product

If unsure, treat it as `full-cycle`.

## Workflow

### Tune

1. Build the pricing contract.
2. Confirm target segment, pricing goal, and current constraints.
3. Adjust the relevant tier, metric, or message.
4. Check rollout risk, evidence strength, and downstream handoffs.

### Full-cycle

1. Build the pricing contract from product, audience, and GTM context.
2. Clarify the pricing goal: growth, conversion quality, expansion, margin, or enterprise capture.
3. Choose the packaging shape, value metric, and price architecture.
4. Map proof, objections, and adoption friction for the proposed model.
5. Plan pricing communication, rollout, and handoffs to sales, launch, docs, and research.
6. Review for claim discipline, fit clarity, and pricing risk before finalizing.

## Output contract

Return only the sections needed for the ask:
- `Pricing goal`
- `Target segment`
- `Current model / constraints`
- `Recommended packaging`
- `Value metric`
- `Price architecture`
- `Message / proof strategy`
- `Objections / risks`
- `Rollout recommendation`
- `Downstream handoffs`
- `Open questions`

## Rules

- Price to value and market reality, not just internal preference.
- Separate packaging, metric, and price point; do not blur them into one decision.
- Keep plan names, tier boundaries, and upgrade logic easy to explain.
- Tie premium claims to features, service levels, or proof that actually exist now.
- Do not invent ROI, savings, willingness-to-pay data, or competitor details.
- Do not present pricing as legal, accounting, tax, or procurement advice.
- When evidence is weak, recommend a test, narrower rollout, or explicit open question instead of false certainty.
- Always state who a tier is for and what behavior should trigger expansion.
