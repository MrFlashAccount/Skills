# Competitor Profiling

Use this skill when the job is to turn competitor evidence into structured profiles that downstream marketing, launch, copy, and sales work can trust.

## Own

- competitor landscape framing from a list of named competitors or URLs
- structured competitor profiles with traceable evidence
- positioning, offer, pricing, packaging, proof, objection, and differentiation analysis
- comparison-ready notes for downstream pages, launches, and sales enablement
- claim-discipline checks around what can and cannot be said about competitors
- explicit strengths, weaknesses, threats, and opportunity framing

## Do not own

- final public comparison-page copy or SEO page production
- speculative attack copy, dishonest feature claims, or legal-risky assertions
- customer persona work not grounded in competitive evidence; use `customer-research` when that is the main job
- pretending stale or incomplete data is current fact

## Default workflow

1. Lock the list of competitors and the decision the research must support.
2. Load shared GTM context so comparison happens against real product truth.
3. Gather evidence from competitor sites, pricing, onboarding surfaces, reviews, community chatter, and available market signals.
4. Normalize each competitor into the same template.
5. Extract differentiators, audience clues, proof, objections, and switch triggers.
6. Separate verified facts, informed inferences, and unknowns.
7. Produce profiles plus a cross-competitor summary or comparison packet.

## Rules

- Facts over dunking.
- Cite the source type for every material claim.
- Separate current product facts from outdated blog copy, roadmap talk, or secondary speculation.
- Be honest where the competitor is stronger.
- Compare against the status quo too, not only named vendors.
- Flag where your own product lacks proof or parity.
- Keep legal and trust risk low: no fabricated pricing, customer counts, or feature gaps.
- If the output will feed a public comparison page, preserve claim discipline explicitly.

## Questions to resolve early

- Which competitors matter most?
- Is the job landscape scan, deep profile, or comparison-prep?
- What downstream decision depends on this: positioning, battlecards, launch, page strategy, or content?
- Do we need a single competitor dossier, side-by-side comparison, or prioritized page plan?

## Deliverables

- competitor profile
- competitive landscape summary
- comparison-ready matrix
- differentiation + objection brief
- page-priority recommendation for downstream comparison content

## Handoffs

- `customer-research`: validate competitor claims against VOC and switcher language
- positioning / messaging skill: turn profiles into strategic differentiation
- copy / comparison skill: write public comparison or alternative pages
- launch skill: use market contrast and objections in rollout framing
- sales-enablement skill: convert findings into battlecards and objection handling
