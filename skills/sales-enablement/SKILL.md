## Routing boundary

Use this skill when the job is:
- creating or revising sales decks, one-pagers, leave-behinds, proposals, demo narratives, or champion materials
- turning positioning into rep-usable talk tracks
- building objection handling tied to proof and fit
- tailoring collateral by persona, stage, segment, or package
- preparing collateral for launch follow-through or pricing conversations

Do not use this skill when the main job is:
- deciding the pricing model itself; use `pricing`
- planning the launch across channels; use `launch`
- writing repository `readme` or technical audience framing; use `devrel-copywriter`
- writing setup, migration, or feature docs; use `docs-writer`
- inventing ROI models or customer proof that has not been validated

## Task class

- `asset`: a bounded asset or localized revision
- `system`: a new sales message set, multi-asset pack, or major collateral refresh across personas/stages

If unsure, treat it as `system`.

## Workflow

### Asset

1. Build the sales-enablement contract.
2. Confirm audience, deal stage, and desired action.
3. Draft the asset around one message spine and the minimum proof needed.
4. Check objection handling and handoffs before finalizing.

### System

1. Build the contract from GTM context, sales motion, and product truth.
2. Define the message spine: pain, differentiation, proof, and next step.
3. Choose the minimum asset set by persona and deal stage.
4. Build collateral outlines and objection coverage.
5. Identify where launch, pricing, DevRel, docs, and customer research must contribute.
6. Review for scan speed, proof discipline, and rep usability.

## Output contract

Return only the sections that fit the ask:
- `Audience / stage`
- `Asset or system goal`
- `Message spine`
- `Recommended assets`
- `Proof to include`
- `Objections to cover`
- `Customization notes`
- `Cross-functional handoffs`
- `Open questions`

## Rules

- Make the asset usable in a live sales context; scannable beats comprehensive.
- Tie every differentiator to buyer impact, not just a feature list.
- Give champions material they can forward internally without rewriting it.
- Keep persona differences explicit: economic, technical, champion, user, blocker.
- Do not invent ROI math, customer quotes, competitor claims, or implementation promises.
- If proof is weak, say what evidence is missing and lower the claim strength.
- Keep next steps concrete and stage-appropriate.
