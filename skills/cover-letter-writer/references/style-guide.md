# Cover letter style guide

## Core purpose
A cover letter is not a biography.
It is a short front-gate message that must earn a reply or a call.

It should answer quickly:
1. Who is this person?
2. Why are they relevant to this role?
3. What practical problem do they solve?
4. Why should I trust them enough to continue?
5. Why should I reply now?

## Required ingredients
Every strong cover letter should contain:
- role + level context
- sweet spot / pain solved
- 1–2 proof points
- a reason for writing to this specific company/post
- a calm CTA

## Problems the letter must solve
The letter should solve:
- reader fatigue / low attention
- uncertainty about candidate fit
- suspicion that the candidate is inflated / "wolf-like"
- lack of obvious business value
- inertia before scheduling a call

## Tone
Default tone:
- short
- human
- calm
- specific
- mature
- non-corporate
- natural enough that it does not read like AI-polished outreach

Avoid:
- hype
- flattery
- begging
- generic passion statements
- long autobiographies
- hero language
- vague buzzwords
- inflated significance language
- salesy confidence
- over-structured "perfect" rhythm

## Anti-AI / humanizer rules
Use these rules every time.

### Remove common AI tells
Avoid phrases and tones like:
- pivotal / crucial / vibrant / testament / underscores / highlights / landscape / key moment
- "I am passionate about..."
- "I would be thrilled..."
- "I believe my experience aligns..."
- empty significance inflation
- corporate wallpaper wording

### Prefer grounded wording
Prefer:
- plain verbs
- direct claims
- concrete facts
- one clean thought per sentence
- believable confidence instead of polished performance

### Keep rhythm human
- mix short and medium sentences
- do not make every sentence equally polished
- let the message feel written by a person, not generated by a template engine

### Keep voice adult, not robotic
Good:
- calm
- direct
- specific
- lightly opinionated when useful

Bad:
- sterile neutrality
- motivational tone
- HR-speak
- overexplaining

### Cover letters should not sound like mini essays
The goal is not literary elegance.
The goal is:
- fast trust
- fast fit recognition
- fast reply

### Avoid dead closing tails
Unless explicitly requested, do not add dead generic endings such as:
- `Резюме: <cv>`
- `Прикладываю резюме`
- `Буду рад ответить на вопросы`
- `Спасибо за внимание`

Prefer ending on a calm, direct CTA.

### Avoid AI-ish vacancy praise
Do not write lines that sound like fake enthusiasm or product-review copy, such as:
- `Вакансия X понравилась`
- `Ваша вакансия выглядит очень близкой`
- `Это не просто еще одна вакансия`
- `Ваш продукт выглядит очень интересным` (unless the user truly wants this angle and there is a real specific reason)

Prefer simpler, more human openings such as:
- `Увидел вашу вакансию и решил написать.`
- `Увидел пост и решил откликнуться.`
- `Решил написать напрямую.`

The opening should feel natural, not performative.

## Anti-"wolf" guidance
When the candidate has strong experience, keep strength but reduce myth-making.

Prefer:
- clear scope
- concrete impact
- grounded wording
- believable proof

Avoid:
- sounding like a superhero in every company
- overusing status brands as the whole argument
- too many big wins in one paragraph
- asking the reader to trust claims without context

## Good structure
A practical structure:
1. Context: why writing / where the post was seen
2. Positioning: who the candidate is
3. Value: what kind of problems they solve
4. Proof: 1–2 examples
5. CTA: calm invitation to talk

## Length
Target:
- usually 450–900 characters in Russian
- can be slightly longer when context is very specific

## Copy-friendly formatting
When generating final ready-to-send versions for chat apps, especially Telegram:
- wrap each finished message in fenced code blocks
- keep commentary outside the code block
- do not mix explanations into the message body
- optimize for one-tap copy/send

## Default style for Sergey
When writing for Sergey:
- keep it short and human
- avoid corporate language
- make it sound adult and capable
- show strong engineering credibility fast
- emphasize hands-on frontend leadership, architecture, delivery, performance, reliability
- do not oversell
- do not sound needy
- do not sound like a fake overpackaged "wolf"

## Example formula
"Привет. Увидел [пост/вакансию]. Я [роль/уровень]. Обычно я полезен там, где нужно [pain solved]. Из последнего: [proof point 1], [proof point 2]. Если вам нужен человек такого профиля, думаю, есть смысл созвониться."
