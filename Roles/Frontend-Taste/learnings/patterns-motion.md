# Pattern Families: Motion

Use this file when the question is about visible motion, transition feel, or whether motion is helping or hurting comprehension.

## Good motion jobs

Motion is most justified when it:
- clarifies cause and effect
- preserves orientation across state change
- improves perceived polish without delaying use
- adds tactile response to interaction

## Motion that usually works

- short structural transitions that explain expansion, collapse, or replacement
- subtle feedback on press, hover, selection, or completion
- ordered reveals when sequence improves comprehension

## Motion that needs caution

- perpetual background motion
- large-area parallax or spectacle motion
- attention magnets near dense data or long-form reading

## Failure modes

- motion that competes with content
- motion that exists only because the screen feels too plain without it
- transitions that are too slow for operational surfaces
- “premium” animation layered on top of weak hierarchy

## Rule of thumb

If the same surface becomes clearer and more confident when the motion is removed, the motion probably was not doing enough real work.
